# Working recipe — Sunday Sourdough

The current reliable method. Update this file when the method changes; the
reasoning lives in LOG.md and memory/.

## Ingredients (per loaf)
- 500 g strong white bread flour
- 350 g water (70% hydration)
- 100 g active starter
- 10 g salt

## Method (24h cold ferment — the default since 2026-07-14)
1. Saturday evening: mix flour and water, rest 30 min (autolyse).
2. Add starter and salt. Stretch and fold 4 times over 2 hours.
3. Shape, place in banneton, cover.
4. Cold-ferment in the fridge overnight (about 24h max).
5. Sunday morning: preheat a covered pot in the oven as hot as it goes.
6. Bake 20 min covered, then 20 min uncovered until deep brown.

## Notes
- Hotter oven + longer proof fixed the pale, dense first attempts.
- Cost is roughly £1.10/loaf; selling at £5.
- One Sunday morning comfortably makes 10 loaves in two oven rounds.
